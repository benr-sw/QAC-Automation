## Slide 8 — The Persian Wars

**THE PERSIAN WARS**

The Persian Empire existed for more than 200 years. It lasted from around 550 to 330 B.C.E. The empire stretched geographically from Greece to India. The Persians came from a region in southwest Asia that is now called Iran. During this time, this region was called Parsa. The Persians were skilled warriors. They had powerful rulers. Though short-lived compared to some, their empire became one of the largest and most powerful in ancient history.

*[Accompanying image: Map of Ancient Persia showing the Persian Empire stretching from Greek City-States and Thrace in the west to India in the east, with Persepolis marked as the capital. Rivers shown include the Tigris and Euphrates. Surrounding bodies of water include the Mediterranean Sea, Black Sea, Caspian Sea, Aral Sea, Arabian Sea, Red Sea, and Persian Gulf.]*

## Slide 17 — Primary Source Analysis: Pericles' Funeral Oration, Part 1

**PRIMARY SOURCE ANALYSIS: PERICLES' FUNERAL ORATION, PART 1**

In 431 B.C.E., Pericles gave a funeral oration, or a formal speech, for all of the soldiers who had died in the Peloponnesian War. His speech focused on Athenian democracy and achievements. Historians believe the speech was recorded and rewritten by Thucydides, a Greek historian. The image is a painting called *Pericles's Funeral Oration* by Phillipp Foltz, done in 1852. Since this speech was written in ancient Greek, there are many different translations. The text is an excerpt from a translation of Pericles's speech.
