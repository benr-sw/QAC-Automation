## Page 5: Political Leagues & The Peloponnesian War — Answer Key

**[Image description: The continuation answer key worksheet with the same chain-link border. The Political Leagues section and Peloponnesian War table are filled in with detailed answers. The Studies Weekly logo appears at the bottom right.]**

### Political Leagues

| The Delian League | The Peloponnesian League |
|-------------------|--------------------------|
| The Delian League was a military agreement between some Greek city-states led by Athens. | The Peloponnesian League was a military alliance between Sparta, Corinth, and their allies. |
| **Cause:** The Persian Wars showed many Greek city-states the need for a unified defense against future threats. | **Cause:** Sparta and Corinth resented the power of Athens under the Delian League. |
| **Consequences:** It contributed to a period of cultural prosperity known as the Golden Age of Athens. It successfully freed Greek cities in Asia Minor and the Aegean Sea from Persian control. The power of Athens led to resentment and conflict with other city-states. | **Consequence:** The Peloponnesian War |

### The Peloponnesian War

| Event | Summary | Cause | Consequence |
|-------|---------|-------|-------------|
| The Peloponnesian War | The Peloponnesian War was a war between the Delian League and Peloponnesian League. It occurred in two stages: The first was 460–446 B.C.E. The second was 431–404 B.C.E. At first, the Athenian navy dominated battles at sea, but eventually the strong Spartan army wore down the Athenians. | The rivalry between Athens and Sparta and each city-state's desire to control the region. | Athens' power and influence were shattered. Sparta won, but both city-states and the entire region were weakened by decades of war. This opened the door for future invasions. |
